 Going over the past month of being enrolled at Flatiron School, I've ran into many bumps and struggles, only to overcome them and keep pushing on. This is going to be a blog on some of the struggles I have had being a full-time working, father and husband while learning here on Flatiron. From day one I sat my wife and kids down and told them "for the next 8-10 months I'm going to put most my free time into school". They were all supportive and happy I was fulfilling my dreams....Until I actually started.
 
  The first week went as smooth as possible, I put in a good amount of work with no one complaing. But since then I have had some things come up. So here are some of the things I've struggled with and what I've done to overcome these struggles. My first big struggle is that for my full-time job I have to get up at 4 A.M. So when I would get home and sit down at my computer I would get pretty sleepy and I felt like I wasn't learning to my full capabilities. So I tried a few things out and set a routine once I get home I walk my dog, hop in the shower and then start learning and that has worked great it gives me a refresher and some energy and I'm learning the way I should.
	
	My second struggle is missing out on doing the things I would normally do with my kids. My son and I really enjoy playing video games together, which we would multiple times a week and now that I'm always studying we just dont get the time to. With my daughter we would watch movies together, play outside, dance around and now it just doesnt happen as often as she would like. I know they are both sad we dont get to do these things as much but with my son I found a single player game he likes and I will study in the room while he plays and hes happy im just there. For my daughter I signed her up for a dance class she's been wanting to do and she couldnt be any happier she loves it. They both know once I am done that everything will go back to normal and it gives me the inspiration to just keep going.
	
	Those are just a few things I have struggled with in the first month but at the end of the day I have the best support from my wife and kids and between them and my passion for coding, no struggle is going to get the best of me!
