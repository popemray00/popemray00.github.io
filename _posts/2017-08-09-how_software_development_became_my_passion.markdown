   I will start off by saying this is my first ever blog post and I'm excited to be blogging from here on out. With that said, here is a little about me. I first learned about coding back in high school around 2004, when I took my first computer class we got into some of the basics and eventually created our own static webpage. I remember at it quickly became my favorite class and I couldnt wait until I had class again. Soon after that class ended I thought that as cool as coding was there where no more options at my school to advance, so I put it behind me and moved on. 11 years later after attending community college but never graduating I went from job to job searching for a career I enjoyed doing, and until October of 2016 I hadn't found it. One day listening to a sports podcast of all things they interview a software developer and he says "About 62% of developers are self-taught" and that instantly brought me back to my high school class memories. He went on to say the website he had learned on and that day when I got home I loggeed into my computer and sign up for a free course. Ever since I heard that developer, coding has became my passion and something I can see myself doing the rest of my life.
	
	
	Since I've started teaching myself back in October, I have taken mulitple free course throughout the web and also a few paid courses. Now, I am a student here at Flatiron and I could not be more excited to learn with the whole community and work with the great instructors. My hopes are to make a career change into the tech industry and help make the web a better place.
